The Future of Living, Working, and Healing

La Ruche is a premium, integrated living environment that brings together housing, behavioral health, wellness, and work into a single, purpose-built ecosystem. In West Hollywood, the model combines co-living, outpatient, and residential clinical services, clinical coworking spaces, and a curated wellness mall and AI platform that includes longevity, recovery, and performance offerings to support people at every stage of health and life. By aligning the environment, community, and care into a single asset, La Ruche replaces fragmented, episodic models with a continuous, experience-driven approach that improves outcomes while generating multiple complementary revenue streams. It is not a treatment center or a coworking space, but a new category of real estate that integrates AI to support living well, working effectively, and sustaining long-term recovery and performance.

Why build all four at once?
Lower cost to acquire customers
A treatment member becomes a co-living resident becomes a club member. You only paid to find them once.
Better outcomes = better business
When members live and socialize in the same ecosystem, recovery rates improve, which drives referrals and reputation.
If one revenue stream slows, others don't
An insurance cut doesn't affect apartment rents. A slow membership month doesn't affect clinical revenue. Diversification protects the whole.

Imagine a catering company. Fixed costs are the kitchen and the salaried chef — you pay those no matter how many events you book. La Ruche is engineered to have mostly variable costs in the clinical business, so if occupancy drops 30%, costs drop nearly 30% too.

Why "variable" clinical costs matter
The therapists and clinicians at La Ruche are contracted per community member, not full-time salaried employees. That means:

• At 50% capacity → clinical staffing costs drop ~50%
• At 100% capacity → full costs, full revenue
• No heavy fixed payroll in a slow month
Key financial engineering in the model
The cost that never changes
Debt service — the total annual payment on all loans — is fixed regardless of revenue. This is the floor every other part of the business must clear before any profit reaches investors.

Capital Stack

The capital stack is how the $78.5M total project cost is financed. Drag the sliders to change the mix of senior debt, mezzanine, and common equity — and watch how it changes debt service, DSCR, and investor returns across every tab.

Think of it like buying a house with three different lenders. The bank (senior debt) lends you the most at the lowest rate but gets paid first if anything goes wrong. A second lender (mezzanine) charges a higher rate because they take more risk. You put in the rest yourself (common equity) — and you get all the upside if the investment succeeds, but you're last in line if it fails.

What today's market actually lends
For a complex mixed-use project with a behavioral health component in 2026, realistic lending parameters are:

• Senior LTC: 50–60% from a traditional bank
• Mezzanine: 10–15% from a debt fund at 10–14%
• Common equity: 25–40% from investors

The original model assumed 63% senior with no mezz — achievable, but optimistic for this asset type. Try setting senior to 55% and mezz to 8% to model a more conservative capital stack and see how DSCR and returns change.

The four assumptions that matter most
Drag any of these down and watch what breaks first.
1
Clinical occupancy
The clinic is 63% of total revenue. If occupancy stays below 70% — which can happen during early licensing, in slow referral networks, or due to insurance denials — free cash flow turns negative. Drag clinical occupancy to 65% and watch the DSCR fall.
2
Average daily rate from insurance
If insurance companies push the rate from $750/day to $500/day, realistic for Medi-Cal or lower-tier plans, clinical revenue falls by $6.25M, wiping out nearly the entire profit margin. Private-pay members at $2,000+/day are the premium that protects the model.
3
Mezzanine debt cost
Adding mezzanine debt to reach a higher LTC increases annual debt service significantly. At 12% interest-only on $10M of mezz, that's $1.2M more in fixed annual payments regardless of revenue. Try setting senior to 55% and mezz to 10% to see the impact on DSCR.
4
Time to stabilization
Every month of delay in getting licensed or reaching full occupancy costs approximately $500K in projected revenue, while all loan payments keep running. A 6-month slip in the licensing timeline adds $3M in cumulative cash burn before the first clinical dollar is earned.
